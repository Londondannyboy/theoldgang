### RESOLVED — Sarah's Father
**Decision:** Sarah's father was NOT a suicide. He was MURDERED. His body was never found. He was buried — in the same unconsecrated ground that the regeneration is now drilling into. The official story was suicide, but it was a cover-up by the Corrupt Policeman and the institutional chain. When the drills disturb the ground, they bring him back — just like the Old Gang. Sarah's journey toward her father is not metaphorical. She literally meets him. The Seventh Seal conversation is REAL — Sarah sits with her dead father, returned from the grave, having the conversation they never had. This is the emotional climax of Book 1.
**Affects:** The entire personal subplot. The ending. The Seventh Seal concept. The relationship between Sarah and the Old Gang (her father IS one of them now). The Corrupt Policeman's crime escalates from cover-up to murder.

### RESOLVED — Point of View
**Decision:** Third person limited, rotating. Sarah is primary POV (~70%). Select chapters from the Camberwell Man, Young Diamond Anne, and possibly modern gangs. This allows the Old Gang to be developed as characters the reader cares about independently of Sarah's investigation.
**Affects:** Structure, chapter allocation, whose interiority we access.

### RESOLVED — Marcus's Arc: Absorbed into the Old Gang
**Decision:** Marcus does NOT remain a separate modern gang leader. He is ABSORBED into the Old Gang through circumstance. He fights to protect someone in his gang against the Old Gang — and he EQUALS them. The Old Gang see someone who matches them for the first time in the modern world. Marcus discovers he belongs with them. He becomes the Old Gang's leader — the top boy — because he's the only living person who can stand in their world.
**The parallel:** Just like Diamond Anne tried not to be Old Gang and was forced into it. Just like the Camberwell Man tried to be alive and was already dead. Marcus tried to be the one who got out — built a legitimate operation, the smart gangster, the businessman. And the Old Gang pulled him back in. "Just when I thought I was out, they pull me back in." The inevitability of circumstance. The system creates the same people, century after century.
**What this means:** Marcus is the MODERN Old Gang. He is the living proof that the conditions haven't changed — that Elephant still produces the same people the rookeries did. He becomes their leader not because he chose it but because the world made him. Same as Diamond Anne. Same as all of them.
**Affects:** Marcus's entire arc, the Old Gang's leadership question (RESOLVED — Marcus leads), the turf clash scene (transforms from confrontation to recruitment), Book 2 (Marcus as living leader of a dead gang)

### TBC — The Corrupt Policeman's Redemption, Death, and Return
**Decision:** The Corrupt Policeman gets a redemption arc, NOT institutional destruction. He inherited the corruption — born into the chain, same estates, same poverty that produces both policemen and criminals (often in the same family). He didn't choose it the way Soames chooses continuation. Sarah's investigation forces him to face what he is. He does one good thing — testimony, evidence, breaking the chain — and then he dies. Suicide on a bridge, the shadow of Sarah's father watching. Not horror — sadness. Two men connected by murder, one already dead, one about to be. Because he's buried in Elephant ground, he comes back as Old Gang. In Book 2: the murderer walking alongside the man he murdered. Sarah's father and his killer in the same gang. The dead don't choose their company. The unconsecrated ground doesn't sort righteous from guilty. The Old Gang don't forgive him. Sarah's father doesn't forgive him. But they're stuck together. This gives Sarah the most painful thing in Book 2 — her father's killer among her father's people. She can't punish him. Death already did. She can't separate them. The ground decided. He needs to be a fuller character in Book 1: a name (TBD), a visible backstory, moments where the human is visible under the institutional mask. A scene where he's among criminals and it's obvious to the reader he's one and the same — from the same streets, same families, some became police, some became criminals. ### TBC — The Political Dimension: Democratic Exclusion
**Decision:** The political dimension is handled through CHARACTER (primarily the Avenger), not as a separate subplot. The Old Gang were excluded from democracy — working-class men without property could not vote until the Reform Acts progressively widened the franchise (1832, 1867, 1884, and fully in 1918). The system that exploited them was the system that excluded them from changing it. The guilds didn't just suppress innovation — they controlled Parliament, set the laws, decided who could vote. The Avenger's philosophical rage includes this: "They made the laws that kept us poor. They kept us too poor to change the laws. That's not corruption. That's architecture."
**The modern observation:** The Old Gang return to a world where everyone can vote — and nothing has changed. Same people run things. Same streets are poor. Same exploitation continues. Universal suffrage didn't fix what they died from. This is texture, not plot — a line, a look, a silence at a polling station.
**The suffrage nuance:** Women with property could vote in local elections before 1835 (Municipal Corporations Act explicitly excluded them). Working-class men didn't get the full vote until 1918 — the same act that gave women over 30 the vote. The suffragette movement was about both gender AND class, but history simplified it. This is a potential historical fact after a chapter.
**The wars:** The Old Gang predate WWI and WWII. The British Legion Old Boy bridges this — born ~1931, his father fought in WWI, he may have served himself. When the Old Gang meet him, they're meeting the century they missed — wars, votes, NHS, welfare state. Everything supposed to fix what killed them. And here he is, penniless, dying alone in a pub. The fixes didn't fix him either.
**Affects:** The Avenger's characterisation (deepened further), historical facts pool, the British Legion Old Boy's function as bridge between eras, Book 2 thematic scope.
