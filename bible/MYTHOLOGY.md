# Mythology & Symbolism

Unlike The Besa (which has an Arthurian layer), The Old Gang's mythology is HISTORICAL rather than legendary. The supernatural is grounded in real facts, real places, real traditions.

## The Wake
The word "wake" comes from watching the dead in case they could be woken. Construction machinery is the modern wake — industrial noise into unconsecrated ground. The tradition works.

## Booth's Map as Sacred Geography
The Old Gang are bound to the streets Booth coloured black. This is not arbitrary — these were the rookeries, the criminal zones. The map is simultaneously historical document and supernatural boundary.

## The Flower
Flowers represent innocence and the sensory world the Old Gang were denied. Old Diamond Anne dies reaching for one — reaching for the girl before the blood.

## The Pineapple
Once the ultimate luxury (rented for display), now £1.50 in Tesco. The gap between their world and ours in a single fruit.

## The Colour Purple
Once impossible (Tyrian murex, colour of royalty). Now everywhere. They wear it as worship, not gang colours.

## The Globe Theatre
Built to remember their world. They sit in it and weep. The past watching a recreation of itself.

## The Necropolis Railway
A railway for the dead, still connected to the living network. Infrastructure built for death, used for death again.

## Diamond Anne's Rings
The same rings on young hands and old hands. Time collapsed into jewellery.

## The London Stone
The oldest thing in the city. A meeting point older than any institution. Where the dead meet because it predates their deaths.
