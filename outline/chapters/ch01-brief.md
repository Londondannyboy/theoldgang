# Chapter 1 Brief — Sarah Arrives

## POV: Sarah (Third Person Limited)

## Summary
Sarah's first day at Elephant and Castle police station. Establish the area (demolition, regeneration), the station (modern, dismissive colleagues), and plant the seeds of her father's connection to this place.

## Key Beats
1. Sarah arrives at the station — describe Elephant: cranes, hoardings, estates coming down, new builds going up
2. The station interior — modern, busy, institutional. She meets her DCI, is assigned a desk.
3. Colleagues — establish the dismissive dynamic. She's the new girl. Polite but not warm.
4. The Corrupt Policeman — first appearance. Senior, patronising. "Welcome aboard." Nothing overtly threatening yet — just power.
5. First mention of the old police unit — a corridor nobody uses, a door with old signage. Someone jokes: "Don't go down there."
6. The father connection — Sarah's internal thought. Why did she choose this station? (Or was it assigned?) Plant without revealing.

## Sensory Details
- The contrast between construction noise outside and institutional hum inside
- The smell of the station (new carpet, coffee, cleaning products) vs. the smell that drifts from the old corridor
- Sarah's awareness of the area — she knows this place, or her father did

## Chapter Ends On
Her first case file landing on her desk. A body near a demolished estate.

## Continuity Notes
- Do NOT reveal father's full story yet — just plant
- Do NOT introduce supernatural elements — this is a normal first day
- Establish Sarah's class straddling — polished but with estate roots audible if you listen
- The station should feel real — not sinister yet, just institutional
